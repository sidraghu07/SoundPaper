macOS · live wallpaper

A desktop wallpaper that listens to whatever's playing.

SoundPaper reads the system audio straight out of Spotify or Apple Music and turns it into one of five generative scenes

Download for macOS macOS 13+ · Apple Silicon & Intel

Five ways it listens

Each scene reads bass, melody, vocals, and beat differently — pick one from the menu bar, any time.

Installing it

  1. Open the downloaded SoundPaper.dmg and drag SoundPaper onto the Applications shortcut in the same window.
  2. Open it from Applications. macOS will block it the first time — see the note alongside for exactly how to get past that.
  3. When prompted, grant Screen Recording permission in System Settings → Privacy & Security. This is what lets SoundPaper hear your music
  4. Quit and reopen SoundPaper once the permission is granted — it only takes effect on the next launch.
  5. Use the menu bar controls to choose Spotify or Apple Music as the source, and pick a scene.

Why macOS blocks it first

SoundPaper is signed but not notarized through Apple, so Gatekeeper flags it as an unidentified developer.

macOS Sonoma / Sequoia (14 & 15)
  • Try opening it once
  • Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, find the message about SoundPaper, and click Open Anyway.
  • Confirm once more when it relaunches.
macOS Ventura and earlier
  • Right-click (or Control-click) the app and choose Open.
  • Click Open in the dialog that appears.
